WHAT SHOULD YOU DO DURING A HEATWAVE?

THE THREE BASIC PRINCIPLES TO BE FOLLOWED DURING AN HEATWAVE

1.FIND AIR CONDITIONING AND MOVE THERE

2.WEAR LIGHT CLOTHING

3.DRINK PLENTY OF FLUIDS AND STAY HYDRATED

DON'T LEAVE PETS OR PEOPLE IN CAR DURING HEAT WAVE.

**The most important thing to remember is DON'T PANIC!!!

If you are in a house

■ Our first instinct on a lovely summer’s day is to open the curtains and let the sunshine in. However, you’re essentially transforming your home into a greenhouse by doing so.

■ Keep your curtains or blinds closed throughout the day. If you want something a little more heavy duty, consider getting some black out blinds to help keep the heat out.

■ An electric fan might seem like the obvious go to when temperatures start to rise but there’s a helpful trick you can use to make it even cooler. Place a bowl of icy water in front of the fan. As the ice evaporates, it will make the air feel pleasantly cool.

If you are in a car

■ Scorching hot days aren’t friendly to your car battery. So before summer weather hits, have your battery tested to ensure it’s in tiptop shape.

■ During a heat wave, it’s especially important to check your fluids since they help to cool down the engine. This includes motor oil, brake fluid, transmission fluid and power steering fluid.

■When you’re looking for a parking place, try to find a shady spot or a parking area that’s underground or shielded from direct sunlight. Sunlight and hot temperatures can fade your vehicle’s exterior

If you are on the Beach?

■ Be aware of heat illness as heat illness occurs when the body cannot cool down. The most serious forms of heat illness are heat exhaustion and heat stroke.

■Make sure you avoid strenuous activity, especially during the sun’s peak hours of 11 a.m. to 4 p.m. On Sunday, dangerous temperatures are expected to last until 9 p.m.

■ Due to the heat wave, the city Parks Department extended the hours of its pools citywide. Prevent heat illness by staying in an air-conditioned place.

Prepare before a Heat Wave

■Insulate your ceiling, walls and underfloor, and sealing draughts to stop cool air from escaping

■ Install external sunshades or awnings, or planting deciduous plants near windows

■ Replacing halogen and incandescent bulbs with LED lights to reduce heat - LED globes can last 30 times longer and use a fraction of the electricity of older-style globes

■ Double-glazing windows or choosing less expensive options, such as secondary glazing, window films or blinds

■ Installing quiet ceiling fans with high-efficiency motors

■ Using energy-efficient appliances, such as televisions, fridges and ovens, that produce less heat.

QUICK TIP

Drink plenty of water. Stay out of direct sunlight. Stay in an air-conditioned room, if possible.

DEMONSTRATION ON BUILDING HEATWAVE RESISTANT BUILDINGS

FOLLOW THESE STEPS TO BUILD YOUR HEATWAVE RESISTANT BULDING

Although many of today’s brand-new homes are safer than older homes, it’s not a rule that you must start from scratch. By performing a few must-have modifications it’s possible to increase your home’s protection from lightning.

Protecting against the sun
Limit the “greenhouse” effect. Draw curtains, lower blinds and close shutters, especially in rooms that are exposed to the sun. Choose pale colours. They will reflect both the light and the heat, rather than absorbing them. Keep bedrooms in the dark during the day.

Protect your windows from heat
Fit protective films to your windows. They will filter the UV rays and reduce the effect of the sun in living rooms but still let the light in.

Keeping the heat outside
Have your roof insulated, especially if you have bedrooms under the eaves. Properly insulated walls will protect you against the heat as well as against the cold. Keep your windows closed when it’s hot. Switch off unnecessary heat sources: Chargers, even when they are not charging, devices on stand-by and computers all give off a fair amount of heat – unplug anything that could be an unwanted heat source. Do not do any cooking in the oven and stick to cold dishes.

Letting cool air in
Air at night and create draughts to ensure that the cool air circulates around the house. Use a ceiling fan to stir up the hot air that has gathered overhead; this will eliminate some of the humidity created due to condensation and sweat.

Hack the heat without AC
If you're eco-conscious then you'll want to stay away from air conditioning - which, thanks to the immense amounts of electricity needed to power them, have been linked to greenhouse gas emissions.But in 40C heat, this is easier said than done.
